My time in Wales so far!

Coming to Wales has certainly been an adventure. There are a lot of things that I find myself being used to now that at first amazed me. For example, I now look right, left, right when crossing a road instead of left, right, left as I would in America.

I also am less fazed now by the British accent. For example, when I'm overhearing a conversation or speaking to a clerk, it sounds almost as if they are American to me now until I actually pay attention to how they sound.

Food is a very important part of my travel experience no matter where I am. Here are some of my favorites so far:

Tortilla:

These burritos are very delicious and are the closest thing I have been able to find to American burritos. They use very fresh ingredients and have a very friendly staff as well.

Five Guys:

I never really ate Five Guys in America and I wouldn't call myself a huge burger person. But I must say, the milkshakes as well as the cajun seasoned french fries at this place are enough to keep me going at least once a week as a weekend treat. It also feels good sometimes to be in a restaurant that reminds me so much of home like Five Guys.

Chai Street Cafe:

This is a fantastic Indian restaurant that I found my second day in Cardiff. It is right next to Cardiff Castle within an alley of restaurants. I highly reccomend their Tikka Masala

Activities:

Over our time here, the study abroad group and I have been on some breath taking excursions:

Chepstow Castle:

Chepstow Castle is definitely one of the coolest places I've been in my life. I didn't even realize that they could make castles as high up as they made this one. I would love the chance to revisit Chepstow and I highly reccomend anyone in Wales do the same.

Tintern Abbey

Tintern Abbey is a gorgeous church built in the 12th century. It amazed me how well preserved this structure was given that some parts of it were so thin looking. For example, I was surprised to see so many of its arches remaining intact despite going through nearly a thousand years of weathering.

Witherspoons Pub: Prince of Wales

The group and I had a nice Saturday evening at one of the pubs in the city center called Witherspoons or, as the locals call it, "Spoons." It is a great atmosphere with friendly people everywhere and affordable food and drinks.
